AIR COLLABORATIVE - PROJECTS - ACCESS RUTGERS GARDENS

Students Propose Safe Access to Nature for All


Event
Apr 27, 2022

Access Rutgers Gardens is a follow-up project to the March2RUGardens. Rutgers Gardens comprise 180-acres of land and feature various gardens and plant collections, a farmers’ market, and natural habitats. This valuable resource is cut off from the campus through US Highway 1 and Ryder's Lane and only accessible by car.

Students of Civil and Environmental Engineering of the Rutgers School of Engineering and Douglass Residential College students worked on proposals for ADA accessible pedestrian-bicycle paths from Cook campus to Rutgers Gardens and a bridge over Ryder´s Lane. The results have been presented with plans and the process was followed by Rutgers students of documentary film.

Collaborators

Dr. Husam Najm • Dr. Nenad Gucunski • Dr. Wolfram Hoefer • Dr. Lauren Errickson

Sponsors

School of Environmental and Biological Sciences • School of Engineering

Impact: Students delivered concrete plans on how to connect Cook campus and Rutgers gardens through pedestrian-bicycle paths, and they designed three variants of a bridge over Ryder´s Lane: a truss bridge, a girder bridge, and a steel cable bridge. They also designed the right ramps and calculated the cost.
