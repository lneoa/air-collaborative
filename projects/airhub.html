AIR COLLABORATIVE - PROJECTS - AIR HUB

A Space for Community Belonging


Space Activation

The AIR collaborative wants to activate under-utilized spaces through creative-placemaking, embodied movement, and community collaboration. Building on a foundation of scholarship in landscape architecture, history, and urban planning, we want to curate publicly-accessible spaces where community members can bridge literal and perceived boundaries. Using grounded theory methods in collaboration with community partners, we would host a series of community dialgoues about spatial justice and knowledge production, building off of regional histories, and plan a series of community walks that invite collective determination of public spaces and communal practices.

Objective

We want to identify an abandoned or underused plot in New Brunswick, where we built a small garden / gathering place for the community. This place shall activate spontaneous or planned gatherings and enhance social connections and well-being.

Collaborators

coLAB Arts • Elijah's Promise • Rutgers • Esperanza Neighborhood

Sponsors

DRC (Douglass Discovery Program) • Bonner Program

Impact: Plant a garden and built an open air gathering place that communioty members enjoy going to. It should be a place for new encounters, new dialogues, for fun activities. The new place should enhance a sense of belonging in a community.
